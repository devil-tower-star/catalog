Two Tears in a Bucket

I could not believe my misfortune. I would have gotten my fortune told by the lady who runs the tarot shop like I do every week, but she’s been missing for a while. Oh, I could not believe my bad luck. If only I had known that I was unlucky this week, I wouldn’t have bought this lotto ticket and stapled it to my suitcase. Oh.. I could not believe the terrible fate that had overcome me.
I was at the office today, finishing up for the day when I realized that new folders were created on my computer. Upon closer inspection I realized they weren't new folders but my old ones that had been emptied out. Alas, if only I could have forseen this tragic turn of events and done something to prevent it, but I was simply too unlucky.
I went to the mall in the evening to get pretzels with a friend. Tomorrow I was leaving for another city so this was going to be the final time I would be able to spend time with them. While waiting and timidly chewing my soft pretzel rolls (some were dipped in cheese), I received a call from said friend, saying that they had come down with the most intense case of diarrhea they've ever experienced and could not come to meet me.
"What an awful turn of events," I thought while eating the pretzel rolls I had saved for them.

The other night, a brief but profound image flashed through my mind suddenly. I began to wonder what my life would look like if I could singlehandedly prevent all the tragedies that ever happened in the world. But if I changed one thing, another thing that I maybe liked would probably change too, so if I had the power to change only the things I didn't like and somehow keep the rest the same, maybe that would be good.
